SHRIMP AND BROCCOLI SKILLETS

Description

Shrimp and Broccoli Skillets are a quick and flavorful dish that combines succulent shrimp with crisp-tender broccoli in a delicious sauce. This recipe is perfect for a busy weeknight dinner as it comes together in a matter of minutes. With its vibrant colors and delightful flavors, this dish is sure to become a family favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1/2 cup low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch (optional, for thickening the sauce)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Sesame seeds and chopped green onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the minced garlic and red pepper flakes (if using) to the skillet. Saut√© for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the shrimp to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes per side until pink and cooked through. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add the broccoli florets and cook for 3-4 minutes until they turn bright green and become crisp-tender.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the chicken or vegetable broth, soy sauce, honey or maple syrup, and cornstarch (if using) until well combined.
  6. Pour the sauce mixture into the skillet with the broccoli. Cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly.
  7. Return the cooked shrimp to the skillet and toss to coat them in the sauce.
  8. Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning as needed.
  9. Remove the skillet from the heat and garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions, if desired.
  10. Serve the shrimp and broccoli skillets hot over steamed rice or noodles.

Tips

  • You can add additional vegetables such as bell peppers, mushrooms, or snap peas to the skillets for extra flavor and texture.
  • For a spicier kick, increase the amount of red pepper flakes or add a dash of sriracha sauce to the sauce mixture.
  • Feel free to substitute the shrimp with chicken, tofu, or your preferred protein.
  • To save time, you can use pre-cooked shrimp and add them to the skillet during the last few minutes of cooking to warm through.
  • Serve the skillets with a squeeze of fresh lemon or lime juice for a burst of citrus flavor.
