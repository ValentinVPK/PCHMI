WHITE BEAN AND KALE SOUP

Description

White Bean and Kale Soup is a hearty and nutritious dish that is packed with flavor and wholesome ingredients. This comforting soup combines creamy white beans, tender kale, and aromatic herbs and spices. It's a satisfying meal on its own or can be served as a delicious starter or side dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cans (15 ounces each) white beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 bunch kale, stems removed and leaves chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh lemon juice (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced onion, minced garlic, diced carrots, and diced celery to the pot. Saut√© for about 5-7 minutes until the vegetables start to soften.
  3. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring it to a boil.
  4. Add the white beans, chopped kale, dried thyme, dried rosemary, and smoked paprika to the pot.
  5. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the soup simmer for about 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together and the kale to wilt.
  6. Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning as needed.
  7. If desired, squeeze fresh lemon juice into the soup just before serving to add a bright and tangy flavor.
  8. Serve the white bean and kale soup hot, garnished with additional herbs or a drizzle of olive oil if desired.

Tips

  • You can customize the soup by adding other vegetables like diced tomatoes, bell peppers, or potatoes.
  • If you prefer a creamier texture, you can use an immersion blender to partially blend the soup, leaving some chunky beans and vegetables.
  • Serve the soup with crusty bread or a side salad for a complete and satisfying meal.
  • Leftover soup can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. Reheat gently on the stovetop or in the microwave.
  • Feel free to experiment with different herbs and spices to suit your taste preferences. Fresh herbs like thyme or rosemary can be used instead of dried.
