SMASHED POTATOES

Description

Smashed Potatoes are a delightful side dish that combines the creamy texture of mashed potatoes with the crispy edges of roasted potatoes. This recipe results in tender, flavorful potatoes with a golden crust. It's a versatile dish that pairs well with various main courses and is sure to be a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds baby potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Place the baby potatoes in a large pot and cover them with cold water. Add a generous amount of salt to the water.
  3. Bring the water to a boil over high heat and cook the potatoes until fork-tender, about 15-20 minutes.
  4. Drain the potatoes and let them cool slightly.
  5. Place the boiled potatoes on the prepared baking sheet and use a potato masher or the bottom of a glass to gently flatten each potato until it is about 1/2-inch thick.
  6. In a small bowl, mix together the olive oil, minced garlic, dried rosemary, dried thyme, salt, and black pepper.
  7. Drizzle the seasoned olive oil mixture over the smashed potatoes, ensuring they are evenly coated.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven for about 20-25 minutes or until the potatoes are golden and crispy.
  9. Remove from the oven and let the smashed potatoes cool for a few minutes.
  10. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley, if desired, before serving.

Tips

  • Feel free to experiment with different herbs and spices to suit your taste preferences. Fresh herbs like thyme or rosemary can be used instead of dried.
  • For added flavor, you can sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese or shredded cheddar cheese on top of the smashed potatoes before baking.
  • Serve the smashed potatoes as a side dish with grilled meats, roasted vegetables, or as a tasty appetizer.
  • Leftover smashed potatoes can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2-3 days. Reheat them in the oven or a skillet to regain their crispiness.
  • If you prefer a softer texture, you can boil the potatoes until they are fully cooked instead of keeping them fork-tender.
