VEGETARIAN CHICKPEA STEW

Description

This hearty and flavorful Vegetarian Chickpea Stew is a delicious and nutritious dish that is perfect for a comforting meal. Packed with protein-rich chickpeas and an array of vegetables, this stew is not only satisfying but also easy to prepare. It's a great option for vegetarians or anyone looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into their diet.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cans (15 ounces each) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup frozen or fresh spinach
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 5 minutes until softened and translucent.
  3. Add minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add diced carrots, celery, and red bell pepper to the pot. Sauté for 5-7 minutes until the vegetables start to soften.
  5. In a small bowl, combine the ground cumin, coriander, smoked paprika, turmeric, and cayenne pepper. Mix well.
  6. Sprinkle the spice mixture over the vegetables in the pot and stir to coat evenly.
  7. Add the diced tomatoes with their juices and the vegetable broth to the pot. Stir well to combine.
  8. Bring the stew to a simmer and let it cook for about 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  9. Add the drained and rinsed chickpeas to the stew. Stir and continue to simmer for another 10 minutes.
  10. Add the spinach and cook for an additional 5 minutes until the spinach wilts.
  11. Taste the stew and season with salt and black pepper according to your preference.
  12. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly before serving.
  13. Garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley if desired.

Tips

  • You can customize this stew by adding other vegetables like zucchini, sweet potatoes, or butternut squash. Just adjust the cooking time accordingly.
  • If you prefer a thicker stew, you can mash some of the chickpeas with a fork or blend a small portion of the stew with an immersion blender before adding the spinach.
  • Serve the stew with crusty bread or over cooked quinoa or rice for a complete and filling meal.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. The flavors tend to develop and intensify over time, making it even more delicious as leftovers.
