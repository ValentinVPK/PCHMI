HEALTHY BOLOGNESE

Description

Healthy Bolognese is a nutritious twist on the classic Italian dish, providing a lighter and healthier alternative while still delivering the rich flavors and comforting qualities. This recipe replaces traditional ground meat with lean ground turkey and incorporates a variety of vegetables for added nutrients. It's a delicious and satisfying option for those looking for a healthier pasta sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound lean ground turkey
  • 1 can (14 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (6 ounces) tomato paste
  • 1 cup low-sodium vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Whole wheat or gluten-free pasta, cooked according to package instructions
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish (optional)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion, carrots, celery, and minced garlic to the skillet. Saut√© for about 5-7 minutes until the vegetables soften.
  3. Add the ground turkey to the skillet and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon or spatula.
  4. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, vegetable broth, dried oregano, dried basil, dried thyme, salt, black pepper, and bay leaf.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally to allow the flavors to meld together.
  6. Remove the bay leaf from the sauce before serving.
  7. Serve the healthy Bolognese sauce over cooked whole wheat or gluten-free pasta.
  8. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley, if desired, and sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese.

Tips

  • If you prefer a smoother sauce, you can use an immersion blender or transfer the sauce to a blender or food processor to blend until desired consistency.
  • Feel free to add additional vegetables such as mushrooms, zucchini, or bell peppers to boost the nutritional content of the sauce.
  • Leftover Bolognese sauce can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days or frozen for longer storage. It's a convenient option for meal prep or future pasta dishes.
  • Consider using whole wheat or gluten-free pasta for a healthier alternative, or try serving the sauce over spiralized zucchini noodles or spaghetti squash for a low-carb option.
